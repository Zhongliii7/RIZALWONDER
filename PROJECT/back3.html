  1. Jose Rizal wrote a letter to a group of young women of Malolos on Feb. 22, 1889, in support of their plan of putting up a night school./li>

  2. Rizal's letter to the women of Malolos emphasized reason and supported the education of women so that they may be enlightened, religiousness required reason, without reason there was only religiosity.


  3. This letter was first written in Tagalog by Rizal in 1889 in response to the political outrage caused by twenty young women petitioning for a night school in Malosos so they might study Spanish. It was later published in the Philippine Review, Jan. 1917


  4. Marcelo H. del Pilar wrote to Jose Rizal requesting that he write a letter to the women of Malolos to laud their valiant struggle against friar hegemony.
